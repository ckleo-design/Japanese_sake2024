SHIRAGIKU SHUZO CO., LTD.

The Taiten Shiragiku Kimoto Junmai Omachinanaju is brewed the Kimoto method, an ancient and traditional sake-making technique that involves a laborious process. Combining rice, water, and koji in small tubs, brewers mash the mixture with a specialized pole. This traditional and strenuous process involves two brewers working together, stirring the paste by moving from side to side—a practice obsolete due to modern technology. This method sets the foundation for a robust sake with pronounced acidity and a distinctive wild, gamey body. Best enjoyed at room temperature or gently warmed, it complements savory delights like cheese, chorizo, and prosciutto.

About the Sake brewery:

Established in 1886, the Shiragiku Shuzo bears the name of a quintessential Japanese autumn flower. As sake ripens during autumn, the Shiragiku blooms in full splendor. The prefix "Taiten" commemorates its prestigious honor prize received during Emperor Showa's enthronement ceremony. In Sakaruga town, Okayama prefecture, the beginning of sake brewing season is marked by clouds flowing down the mountainside. In this setting, the autumnal charm of the natural surroundings align with the brewery's legacy to form an enduring tradition amidst Okayama's stunning landscapes.
